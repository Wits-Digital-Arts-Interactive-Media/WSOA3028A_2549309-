Advanced UX Strategies for Modern Web Design

15 March 2024 by Aaminah Habib

Here are a few options of websites i want to inspect and analyse thier design practices

Website Pros Cons
Lindt
  • Brand Experience: Offers insights into how luxury brands use UX/UI design to enhance brand perception and customer experience.
  • Visual Design and Aesthetics: High potential for analyzing the impact of visual design on user experience, especially in creating a premium feel.
  • Niche Product Focus: The focus on a single product type might not provide enough variety in UX/UI challenges and solutions.
  • Limited Functionality: As a product-focused site, it might lack the complexity of user interactions found in service-oriented sites like Discovery or educational platforms.
Superbalist
  • E-commerce Insights: Provides a rich field for analyzing how design influences shopping behavior, customer engagement, and satisfaction.
  • Modern Design Practices: Likely to employ the latest UX/UI trends and technologies, offering a contemporary case study.
  • Focus on Commercial Objectives: The primary focus on sales could overshadow other UX/UI considerations, potentially limiting the analysis of broader design principles.
Department of Labour
  • Government Service Analysis: Offers a chance to explore UX/UI in the context of government services, focusing on accessibility, clarity, and how effectively services are delivered to the public.
  • Ethical Considerations: Strong potential for discussing ethical considerations in design, given the importance of inclusivity and accessibility in government websites.
  • Limited Design Innovation: Government websites might not exhibit the same level of design innovation or aesthetics as commercial sites, potentially limiting the scope of analysis on certain UX/UI trends.
Discovery Health
  • Relevance: As a health insurance platform, it's highly relevant to many users' lives, offering insights into how design affects important decisions.
  • Complex User Journeys: Provides a complex array of user tasks (finding information, submitting claims, etc.), allowing for a rich analysis of UX.
  • Specialized Audience: The focus on health insurance might limit the applicability of findings to broader UX/UI design principles.
  • Complexity: The complexity of insurance services could make it harder to isolate UX/UI elements from the overall service experience.
University of Cape Town (UCT)
  • Diverse User Base: Offers a broad range of content and services, catering to a diverse user base (students, faculty, researchers), which can provide insights into how different needs are addressed.
  • Educational Context: Allows exploration of UX/UI in an educational setting, including accessibility and information dissemination.
  • Niche Focus: The specific focus on education might not fully explore other UX/UI principles applicable in different sectors.
  • Information Overload: Educational websites can suffer from too much information, which might complicate the analysis of design elements.
Mercedes-Benz South Africa
  • Brand and Luxury Appeal: Analyzing a luxury automotive brand like Mercedes-Benz can provide insights into how UX/UI design is used to convey quality, prestige, and exclusivity.
  • High-Quality Visuals and Interactivity: Likely features high-quality images, videos, and interactive elements (e.g., car configurators) that enhance the user experience and engagement.
  • Innovation and Technology Focus: Mercedes-Benz is known for innovation, which might be reflected in the website's design and functionalities, offering a window into how cutting-edge technology is incorporated into UX/UI design.
  • Customer Journey: Offers a complex customer journey, from exploring car models to booking test drives and accessing after-sales services, providing a broad scope for analysis.
  • Niche Market: Focused on a specific, high-income target audience, which might limit the applicability of some UX/UI insights to broader contexts.
  • Complexity in Navigation: The depth of content and range of services (new cars, used cars, financial services, after-sales support) could result in a complex navigation structure, making it challenging to evaluate the effectiveness of design elements without considering the entire ecosystem.
  • High Expectations: The high standards and expectations of the website's target audience might influence the design in ways that are not applicable to other sectors, potentially skewing the analysis of what constitutes "good" UX/UI.
  • While I would not base my entire essay on this website mainly for its cons, adding a little bit about the Mercedes-Benz South Africa website brings a commercial and luxury perspective, complementing the educational, government, health insurance, and retail sectors represented by the other websites. This variety can enrich the essay by showcasing how different sectors prioritize and implement UX/UI design principles and ethical considerations.

Selection Process

Narrowing down my options, I had to choose a few from the above and see in which directions I can explore them.

  • Discovery Health
    • Comprehensive Analysis Potential: Offers a wide range of user interactions and services to analyze, from navigating insurance options to submitting claims online.
    • Ethical Considerations: Health insurance involves significant ethical considerations, especially around data privacy, security, and accessibility, making it a compelling choice for an essay focused on ethics in UX/UI design.
  • Department of Labour
    • Broad User Base and Public Service: Government websites have a mandate to serve a broad and diverse user base, providing fertile ground for discussing inclusivity, accessibility, and ethical considerations.
    • Challenges and Improvements: Likely presents clear opportunities for suggesting improvements, especially regarding accessibility and the ethical handling of user data, which can demonstrate critical engagement and depth of analysis.
  • Mercedes-Benz South Africa
    • Brand Experience and Luxury Market: Analyzing a luxury brand like Mercedes-Benz allows for exploration of how UX/UI design contributes to brand perception, customer loyalty, and the ethical implications of marketing high-end products.
    • Innovation and Technology: Offers insights into how advanced technologies and design innovations are employed to enhance user experience, with a potential focus on ethical considerations related to data use, privacy, and user consent in a luxury context.

Final Choice: Discovery Health

I want to focus on techniques and methods used well in good UI and UX design so that I can learn from them and apply it to my websites. Discovery Health stands out as a particularly suitable choice. It offers a blend of complex user interactions, a wide service range, and significant ethical considerations tied to personal health and data security. This complexity allows for a rich analysis aligned with academic objectives, demonstrating how UX/UI design impacts user behavior and trust, particularly in sensitive areas like health insurance.

Discovery Health's context provides ample opportunity to discuss how ethical design practices can enhance user trust and satisfaction, a crucial aspect of UX/UI analysis. I can explore how effectively the website navigates the balance between innovative design and ethical considerations, such as accessibility, privacy, and data protection. This approach would reflect current discussions in the field of UX/UI design, potentially making my essay more relevant and impactful.
